What is UNDERTALE?

Undertale is a indie videogame made by Toby Fox with a story centering around a human child who falls into an underground world inhabited by monsters. These monsters were banished underground and sealed there after a long war with humans, having been long forgotten. The player must navigate through the underground and interact with the various monsters they encounter, choosing whether to fight or befriend them. The game's unique selling point is its "mercy" mechanic, which allows players to spare enemies instead of killing them. This mechanic leads to multiple branching story paths and multiple endings, depending on the player's choices throughout the game.

Undertale also features a memorable cast of characters, each with their own distinct personalities and backstories. The game's quirky sense of humor and heartfelt moments have resonated with players, leading to a dedicated fanbase and numerous fan works.

Upon its release, Undertale received critical acclaim for its unique storytelling, memorable characters, and innovative gameplay mechanics. It has since become a cultural phenomenon, with its impact felt in the gaming community and beyond. Toby Fox's creation has inspired countless fan works, merchandise, and even a Broadway musical adaptation.


For more information on the game's origin and background, check the Undertale Wiki.
